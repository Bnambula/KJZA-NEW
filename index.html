Luwombo
Uganda’s Royal Steam Stew

Luwombo is one of Uganda’s most treasured dishes — tender meat or chicken slow-steamed inside banana leaves with a rich groundnut sauce. Originally prepared for the Buganda royal courts, it is today a centrepiece at celebrations, weddings and Sunday family meals. The banana leaf wrapping seals in every flavour and gives an unmistakeable earthy aroma.

🛒 Ingredients — all on Kujaza
📋 Method
  1. Prepare leaves: Wilt plantain leaves over a flame for 30 secs until pliable. Wipe clean and set aside.
  2. Marinate: Cut chicken or beef into pieces. Season with onions, garlic, ginger, salt and bilungo paste. Rest 30 minutes.
  3. Make the sauce: Mix ebinyebwa (groundnut paste) with warm water to a thick consistency. Add spring onions, tomatoes, curry powder.
  4. Wrap: Lay a banana leaf flat. Add meat and spoon sauce over it. Fold into a sealed parcel, tie with a leaf strip.
  5. Steam: Stack parcels in a pot over boiling water. Cover tightly. Steam 1.5–2 hours.
  6. Serve: Open at the table for the full aroma. Serve with matooke, rice or sweet potatoes.
💡Kujaza Tip: Use fresh plantain leaves from our store — they are the secret to authentic flavour. Order extra leaves for serving too!

Nsenene (long-horned grasshoppers) are a seasonal Ugandan delicacy anticipated twice a year — November and April. Crispy, salty and packed with protein, they are eaten as a snack or cooked into a full dish with onions and chilli. A true taste of Uganda that surprises and delights every visitor.

🛒 Ingredients — all on Kujaza
📋 Method
  1. Clean: Remove wings and hind legs. Rinse in salted water and drain. Pat dry thoroughly.
  2. Dry roast: Heat a dry pan on medium. Add nsenene, stir constantly for 5 minutes until golden and moisture is released.
  3. Fry with flavour: Add a splash of oil, sliced onions, garlic, chilli and salt. Stir-fry 8–10 minutes until crisp and golden brown.
  4. Finish: Remove from heat. Squeeze lemon juice over and toss with fresh spring onions.
  5. Serve: Eat warm as a snack, or serve alongside matooke and groundnut sauce.
💡Kujaza Tip: Nsenene are seasonal and sell out fast — order early in November and April. We sell them pre-cleaned and ready to cook!

Bilungo is a rich Ugandan stew built on smoked, dried eggplant as its flavour base. The eggplant is charred over fire, peeled and pounded into a paste that gives the sauce a deep, smoky earthiness unlike anything else. Combined with tomatoes, onions, groundnut paste and dodo, it creates one of Uganda’s most distinctive sauces.

🛒 Ingredients — all on Kujaza
📋 Method
  1. Char eggplant: Place whole eggplants on open flame. Char until blackened all over. Cool then peel off charred skin to reveal smoky flesh.
  2. Make bilungo paste: Mash charred eggplant with salt, garlic and chilli into a smooth paste. Or use our ready-made bilungo paste!
  3. Build the sauce: Fry onions and spring onions in oil until golden. Add tomatoes, cook down 8 minutes. Stir in bilungo paste.
  4. Add greens: Stir in dodo or spinach. Add groundnut paste dissolved in water. Add chicken or keep vegetarian.
  5. Simmer: Cook on low heat 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thick and fragrant.
  6. Serve: Over matooke, rice, cassava or sweet potatoes. Garnish with spring onions.
💡Kujaza Tip: We stock pre-prepared bilungo paste — skip the charring step and get straight to cooking! Same authentic smoky flavour.

Frequently Asked Questions

How fast is Express Delivery?▼
Express Delivery gets your groceries to you in 15–25 minutes for Zone 1 areas (Kawempe, Nakawa, Bugolobi). Zone 2 is 15–25 min, Zone 3 is 25–35 min. A dedicated rider is dispatched immediately from the nearest micro-hub.
How do I order via WhatsApp?▼
Send your order list to 0766026401 on WhatsApp. Tell us what you need, your delivery area, and preferred payment method. We confirm and dispatch within minutes.
What payment methods do you accept?▼
We accept MTN Mobile Money, Airtel Money, Cash on Delivery, Visa/Mastercard (online), and Bank Transfer. For B2B clients, we offer 7–30 day credit terms.
What is your freshness guarantee?▼
If any item is not fresh on delivery, we replace it free of charge or give you a full refund within 24 hours. No questions asked. Report via WhatsApp or raise a ticket.
Do you do bulk and B2B orders?▼
Yes! We supply hotels, schools, restaurants and businesses with weekly standing orders, custom quantities, invoicing and credit terms. Email b2b@kujazafresh.com or WhatsApp 0766026401.
Which areas do you deliver to?▼
We deliver across Greater Kampala including Kawempe, Nakawa, Najjera, Ntinda, Makindye, Bugolobi, Kisaasi, Bukoto, Bweyogerere, Wakiso, Kajjansi, Entebbe, Kitende, Lubowa and Mukono.
